About

I work on technology and philosophy.

Ever since making games on my TI-85 in middle school, I knew software was a special domain for unlocking human ingenuity. I vividly remember groking the concept of a Universal Turing Machine in math class, and aweing its profundity.

Then, I studied political philosophy in college because I'm interested in the kind of systems, both institutional and ideal, that foster greater human flourishing.

I combine these core interests as a practicioner-theoretician. I believe philosophy abstracted from action becomes unweildy, so I ground my understanding in practice and implementation.

You can find my more philosophical work on the Agora Politics podcast, a long-form interview show I started in 2020, in writing on my Substack, Hacking State, and on Youtube, if you prefer videos. For academic work, see my Academia page.
